Welcome to CaulieflowerDB

A multi-omics database for cauliflower research community



Brassica oleracea, as the C type diploid in the triangle of U, is well-characterized by its remarkable morphological diversity, bearing specialized leafy, stem, or floral organs represented by Chinese kale, kohlrabi, Brussels sprouts, cabbage, broccoli and cauliflower. The great variety of wild species, the characteristics of intra-crossability and strong self-incomatibility raise serious challenges for investigating the domestication history of B. oleracea, the authentic relationships among subspecies, and the bona fide ancestral source. Recently, multiple evidence indicated that the Aegean endemic B. cretica might be the origin of currently cultivated B. oleracea.

CauliflowerDB provides access to gene assembly visualization, SNP seeking, consensus sequence generating, and genes & CRISPR targets searching. It serves as a valuable tool and resources for the cauliflower research community.
